    Junior Colleges in Pune

    Having 23 subjects in 11 distinct languages with 50 different combinations are pre-university curriculum offered by the various junior colleges of the country. Popularly known as PUC, junior colleges offer intermediate courses before a student moves ahead to pursue their degree course. Junior Colleges in Pune enjoy seats in advantageous areas which can be conveniently accessed from different corners of the city. The intermediate course offered at a junior college is either conducted by one of the state education institutions or recognized boards in India. It is mandatory for any student to clear his or her pre-university education before applying for university courses or higher education. Arts, science and commerce are broadly the three major streams for which students can apply in a junior college. Students are permitted admission into junior colleges based upon marks scored in the 10th examination. Scroll up the page to find a long list of all the reputed Junior Colleges from the locality.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    1. Do junior colleges in Pune provide online admission forms?

    A number of junior colleges have an online as well as offline admission process. You can check about it at the time of inquiry.

    2. What facilities are provided at junior colleges in Pune?

    Generally, all junior colleges provide their students with best infrastructure such as libraries, computer labs, well-equipped classrooms, etc.

    3. Do junior colleges in Pune secure seats for in-house students for degree college admissions?

    If the junior college also offers degree college courses, then they might give first preference to in-house students. Please inquire about this at time of admissions.

    4. Will the college allow me to pay the fees in installments?

    There are many colleges that allow their students to pay the fees in installments. It is suggested you ask about this at time of inquiry.

    5. What is the fees for junior college admission?

    The fees structure will depend on the stream you choose, college in question, board, etc. Please check with the college in question.
